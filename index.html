Study Buddy: Designed by Ada Wang, Annie Chen, Becky Chen, Elizabeth Weaver


Problem Statement

Because of the isolated environment created by online learning, there is an inability for students to study and focus together with other students while taking online classes at home. Furthermore, the variety of websites and virtual meeting platforms teachers and professors use makes it difficult for students to meet, collaborate, and study together outside of classes.


Our Solution

Our application, Study Buddy, is an interactive site where users can find buddies to study with and better organize their resources. In order to support our target users, who are college students taking classs at home, we provide them with the platform to connect with other students at their schools and join unique study rooms that can be created for different purposes. In addition, our website also has special features to help users better organize their study materials and the numerous meeting links they have, which can also help them to better focus on their work by allowing them to quickly find resources they need.

Takeaways

1. User's opinions are critical!

Our team originally thought that we had created an application that is well-designed, but when we tested our prototype with users, some users reported that some designs are confusing. We as designers would not be able to identify these confusing aspects by ourselves.

2. It is important to conduct several tests to evaluate the usability of our design.

Developers might not be able to identify all the flaws in one test. We also received slightly different feedback from different users.

3. Prototypes are useful tools that can help us carry out our design ideas during the initial phases.

During our first presentation with the class, many people had questions regarding our design, especially about our Study Materials page. However, users no longer had these questions after they saw and experienced the simple prototype we created with Balsamiq, which shows that the prototype helped users to visualize some of the design ideas that we could not properly express verbally and helped them to focus on other functionality issues.
