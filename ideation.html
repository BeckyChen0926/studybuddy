Design I rationale

Playful and Engaging:

This design was made keeping young audiences in mind. Design 1 includes a social media aspect.

Detailed:

While the setup will be familiar and intuitive to iPhone users, there is still lots of information on each page.

Creative/Personal:

The design utilizes a variety of icons and interactive features. In addition, users also has the ability to personalize their experience.







Design II rationale

Efficient:

Most of the tasks can be done with a few clicks with this design. Not too many extra, complicated designs are incorporated

Minimalistic:

The design is simple and minimalistic. Not too many designs are present to clutter the screen

Organized

The design is highly organized. Similar functions are grouped together, and functions are divided into subgroups and sub-categories

Summary of takeaways from in-class design review session

Based on the feedback our team received in class, we decided to adopt a design that incorporates aspects from both design directions we presented in class.

Students complimented the first design direction, or the more “playful” design for its color scheme and interactive features. However, people also mentioned that they like the organization and panels used in the second design. As a result, our team has decided to adapt to a design that combines the strengths from both designs.

Our new design for the application will have the interactive and personalizable features in design 1 and well as the organization in design 2. In addition, our new design will incorporate some new features. We will include more labels next to the icons used in the application to make the application more learnable for new users. We will also have a new anonymous tab within each study group or class group for students to ask each other questions. There will also be a kick-out feature in group meetings and video chats that would allow students to kick out users that are disturbing the group meeting or users that do not belong to the group. However, in order for a user to be kicked out of the meeting, at least 50% of the participants in the video meeting would have to agree or vote to kick the user out.

In addition, to prevent errors or users accidentally uploading the wrong material, we will also add a confirmation step to double check with the user before the user can proceed to upload the study material. Users will also be able to delete the study materials they uploaded later. Moreover, to prevent cheating using the application, we will also make the users agree to the Terms and Agreement to acknowledge that they are aware of their school’s Honor Code and will not conduct or participate in activities that are against the Honor Code.
